The Effect of TRAIN Law on SMEs

How the TRAIN Law affects business management for small and medium enterprises.

Sales Management

Implement programs to engage and inform customers about the value and benefits of the products and services offered.

Importance and Benefits:

Builds Customer Loyalty

Highlighting the unique value of products increases trust and satisfaction, leading to repeat customers.

Differentiates the Business

Focuses on quality and benefits instead of price wars, allowing the business to stand out in a competitive market.

Drives Sales Growth

Educated customers are more likely to perceive the products/services as worth the price, leading to increased purchases.

Avoids Price Undercutting

Helps businesses avoid damaging profitability by focusing on value rather than lowering prices.

Cost Management

Study workforce allocation and adopt flexible staffing models.

Importance and Benefits:

Maintains Productivity

Keeping the workforce stable ensures tasks are completed efficiently without overburdening remaining staff.

Increases Employee Satisfaction

Flexible staffing models improve morale by reducing job insecurity and offering better work-life balance.

Reduces Costs

Instead of layoffs, smarter task allocation and flexible scheduling reduce costs without affecting output.

Promotes Long-Term Growth

Retaining experienced staff fosters consistency, enhances innovation, and improves overall business performance.
